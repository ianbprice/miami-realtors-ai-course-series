# Lead Magnet Source Notes

These notes synthesize ideas from two private PDF exports provided by the instructor from an April 21, 2026 real estate AI course. The original PDFs should not be published in this public repository unless redistribution rights are confirmed.

## Source Themes

- Many agents use AI after they already have the lead, such as for listing descriptions, email drafts, and social posts.
- The stronger opportunity is using AI earlier to generate leads through useful, local content.
- Lead magnets should be simple, skimmable, local, and good enough to launch.
- Local content beats generic content because it creates credibility and gives prospects a reason to engage.
- Short guides can become social posts, videos, emails, blog posts, and nurture sequences.
- Follow-up should use soft calls to action that invite a reply instead of forcing a phone call too early.
- Fair Housing review must be part of the prompt and editing process.

## Lead Magnet Models To Keep

### Five Coolest Things

Best for relocation buyers, second-home buyers, visitors, and lifestyle-driven prospects.

Core idea:

- Pick five local attractions, events, activities, or lifestyle hooks.
- Make the guide short and visual.
- Use it as a downloadable PDF or social opt-in.

### Local Guide

Best for showing local expertise.

Core idea:

- Restaurants.
- Coffee shops.
- Parks or things to do.
- Practical local tips.
- Soft CTA for local guidance.

### Town Questions Answered

Best for search-driven content and ongoing social topics.

Core idea:

- Use search-listening tools or real client questions.
- Identify 8 to 12 useful local questions.
- Create clear, compliant answers.
- Repurpose each question into a post, short video, email, or blog topic.

## Tool Notes

- AnswerThePublic can be used for discovering questions people search around a city or topic. Current public documentation says free accounts have daily limits and limited exports, so avoid promising a fixed price or unlimited usage.
- ManyChat can support Instagram comment-to-DM workflows. Current public documentation notes platform limitations, opt-in behavior, and anti-spam best practices, so teach it as an example workflow rather than a guaranteed automation outcome.
- Link-in-bio tools can be used for simple lead capture when paid ads are not part of the strategy.
- Meta ad rules for housing are sensitive and change over time. Any paid-ad instruction should be verified against current Meta policies before teaching.

Useful public docs:

- AnswerThePublic free plan: https://answerthepublic.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/22617503900187-AnswerThePublic-Free-Plan-Features-Limits-Upgrade-Guide
