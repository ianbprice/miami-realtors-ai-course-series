# Responsible AI Use Checklist

Course: AI for Real Estate Sales: From Confusion to Daily Use

AI can help draft, organize, summarize, and prepare. It does not replace professional judgment.

## Before You Prompt

- [ ] Remove private client names, contact information, financial details, access details, negotiation strategy, and confidential transaction context unless you have authorization and proper safeguards.
- [ ] Check brokerage policy for AI tool use.
- [ ] Use verified property, MLS, and market information when facts matter.
- [ ] Keep separate chats or contexts when mixing information could create confusion or privacy risk.

## Before You Use AI Output

- [ ] Review the content yourself before sending, publishing, posting, or adding to a CRM workflow.
- [ ] Verify property facts, price, availability, MLS data, market stats, school references, neighborhood claims, dates, and legal or financial statements.
- [ ] Remove anything AI invented or stated too confidently.
- [ ] Confirm the message fits your voice, local practice, and client situation.

## Fair Housing And Advertising Review

- [ ] Avoid protected-class language.
- [ ] Avoid discriminatory targeting.
- [ ] Avoid steering language.
- [ ] Avoid unsupported neighborhood, safety, school, or demographic claims.
- [ ] Review marketing copy for advertising rules, brokerage requirements, MLS rules, and platform requirements.

## Consumer Communication Review

- [ ] Follow consumer communication rules and opt-out requirements.
- [ ] Do not use AI to auto-send communication unless the tool, permissions, workflow, and brokerage policy clearly allow it.
- [ ] Keep records as required by brokerage policy or applicable rules.
- [ ] Make sure the message clearly represents you and your brokerage where required.

## Professional Judgment

- [ ] Treat AI as a drafting and preparation assistant.
- [ ] Do not rely on AI for legal, financial, tax, appraisal, lending, inspection, insurance, or regulatory advice.
- [ ] When a question requires professional advice outside your role, refer the client to the appropriate professional.
- [ ] If the output feels too certain, too broad, too salesy, or too clever, slow down and revise.

## Quick Review Lens

Ask four questions:

1. Is it true?
2. Is it allowed?
3. Is it fair?
4. Is it yours?
