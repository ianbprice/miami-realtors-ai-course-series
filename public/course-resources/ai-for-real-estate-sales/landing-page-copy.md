## C. Full Prompt Library

Intro copy:
Copy a prompt, replace the placeholders, review the output, verify facts, and edit before sending. The goal is not to sound like AI. The goal is to sound like yourself on a good day.

### Prompt Formula

Use this structure when you need better output:

- **Role:** Who should AI help as?
- **Task:** What should it create or improve?
- **Context:** What facts, notes, lead source, property details, or client situation matter?
- **Audience:** Who will receive it?
- **Tone:** How should it sound?
- **Constraints:** What should it avoid?
- **Format:** Text, email, voicemail, checklist, table, agenda, script, etc.
- **Goal:** What should the message or workflow accomplish?

Base prompt:

```text
You are helping me as a real estate professional. I need to create [TASK] for [AUDIENCE]. Here is the context: [CONTEXT]. The tone should be [TONE]. Avoid [CONSTRAINTS]. Format the result as [FORMAT]. The goal is [GOAL]. Do not invent missing facts. Flag anything I should verify before using this.
```

### Lead Follow-Up

```text
Create a first-response message for a new [LEAD SOURCE] lead. Context: [LEAD NOTES]. They asked about [PROPERTY OR AREA]. Write one text message, one short email, and one voicemail opener. Keep the tone warm, concise, and appointment-oriented. Do not pressure them. Ask one simple next-step question.
```

```text
Review these lead notes and suggest the best next message: [CRM NOTES]. Identify the likely intent level, the missing information I should ask for, and one text message that gives the lead a reason to respond.
```

### Buyer Consultation Prep

```text
Help me prepare for a buyer consultation. Buyer context: [BUYER GOAL], [PRICE RANGE], [AREAS], [TIMELINE], [FINANCING STATUS], [CONCERNS]. Create a short meeting agenda, five discovery questions, likely objections, and a follow-up message I can send after the appointment.
```

### Seller Consultation Prep

```text
Help me prepare for a listing consultation. Seller context: [SELLER MOTIVATION], [TIMELINE], [PROPERTY DETAILS], [KNOWN UPDATES], [PRICING EXPECTATION], [CONCERNS]. Create a meeting agenda, discovery questions, talking points, and a follow-up email. Do not make pricing claims without verified comparable sales.
```

### Listing Description Drafts

```text
Draft a listing description using only these verified facts: [PROPERTY FACT BLOCK]. Tone: [TONE]. Audience: [TARGET BUYER TYPE WITHOUT PROTECTED-CLASS LANGUAGE]. Do not invent features, school claims, neighborhood claims, square footage, views, upgrades, or amenities. Flag missing details I should verify.
```

### Social Media Post Drafts

```text
Turn this verified property fact block into three social media captions: [PROPERTY FACT BLOCK]. Create one professional caption, one short casual caption, and one open-house caption. Avoid Fair Housing risk, unsupported claims, and exaggerated language.
```

### Email Follow-Up

```text
Rewrite this email so it sounds like a helpful real estate agent, not an automated drip: [DRAFT EMAIL]. Keep it under [WORD COUNT] words, make it specific to [CLIENT OR LEAD CONTEXT], and end with one clear next-step question.
```

### Text Message Follow-Up

```text
Create a short text message for [CLIENT NAME OR LEAD TYPE] based on this context: [CONTEXT]. Keep it natural, under 320 characters, and focused on one next step. Avoid sounding pushy or generic.
```

### Open House Follow-Up

```text
Create follow-up messages for an open house visitor. Visitor notes: [NOTES]. Write a same-day text, a next-day email, and a short phone opener. The goal is to learn whether they are actively searching and whether a buyer consultation or showing makes sense.
```

### Past Client Reactivation

```text
Write a warm reactivation message to a past client. Context: [PAST CLIENT CONTEXT], [LAST CONTACT], [LOCAL MARKET HOOK]. Keep it personal, useful, and low-pressure. Include one simple question that invites a reply.
```

### Sphere Nurturing

```text
Create a monthly sphere email based on this topic: [TOPIC]. Audience: homeowners and local contacts. Tone: practical and conversational. Include one useful insight, one local prompt for conversation, and one soft offer to help. Avoid market predictions and unsupported claims.
```

### Objection Handling

```text
Role-play as [BUYER OR SELLER] who says: "[OBJECTION]." Push back realistically. After each response I write, tell me what sounded clear, what sounded defensive, and how I can improve. Keep the advice compliant and avoid legal, financial, or predictive claims.
```

### Daily Task Planning

```text
Help me prioritize my real estate work today. Here are my tasks: [TASK LIST]. Here are my active clients and leads: [SUMMARY]. Group the day into morning, midday, afternoon, and end-of-day. Identify three high-value follow-up actions and one task I can simplify with AI.
```

### CRM Cleanup

```text
Turn these rough CRM notes into a clean summary and next step: [NOTES]. Format as: client goal, timeline, important context, last interaction, next best action, message draft, and facts to verify. Remove sensitive details that should not be shared externally.
```

### Content Repurposing

```text
Repurpose this market update or client education note into three formats: [SOURCE CONTENT]. Create a short email, a social caption, and a 45-second video outline. Keep the facts unchanged and flag any claims that need verification.
```

### Market Update Explanation

```text
Explain this market information in plain language for [BUYER/SELLER/PAST CLIENT]: [VERIFIED MARKET DATA]. Avoid predictions, legal advice, financial advice, and exaggerated claims. Include what this may mean generally and what should be reviewed with a qualified professional if needed.
```

### Compliance Review Checklist Prompt

```text
Review this real estate message for risk before I use it: [DRAFT]. Check for unsupported facts, Fair Housing concerns, discriminatory language or targeting, confidential information, MLS or advertising issues, pricing or market overclaims, financial/legal advice, and tone problems. Return a table with risk, why it matters, and safer wording.
```

### Quick Review Before Use

- Did I remove private or confidential information before prompting?
- Did I provide verified facts instead of asking AI to guess?
- Did I review the result for accuracy and tone?
- Did I check Fair Housing, MLS, advertising, brokerage policy, and opt-out requirements where relevant?
- Does the final version sound like me on a good day?

## D. 7-Day Implementation Plan

Intro copy:
Use this plan to turn the class into one repeatable habit. Keep the first week simple: one workflow, one prompt, one review habit.

### Day 1: Pick One Workflow

- **Goal:** Choose where AI will help first.
- **Task:** Select one repeated task from your normal week.
- **Tool/prompt to use:** Daily task planning prompt from the prompt library.
- **Expected outcome:** One practical workflow to test.
- **10-minute action step:** Write down three tasks you delay because they are repetitive, awkward, or time-consuming. Pick one.

### Day 2: Build Your First Reusable Prompt

- **Goal:** Turn the workflow into a repeatable prompt.
- **Task:** Use the real estate prompt formula.
- **Tool/prompt to use:** Role, task, context, audience, tone, constraints, format, and goal.
- **Expected outcome:** One saved prompt you can reuse.
- **10-minute action step:** Write the prompt with placeholders such as [CLIENT NAME], [LEAD SOURCE], [PROPERTY FACTS], and [NEXT STEP].

### Day 3: Test It On A Low-Risk Draft

- **Goal:** Practice without exposing private information.
- **Task:** Use synthetic or sanitized notes to generate a draft.
- **Tool/prompt to use:** Email follow-up or text message follow-up prompt.
- **Expected outcome:** A usable draft and a list of edits.
- **10-minute action step:** Run the prompt once, then mark what you would change before sending.

### Day 4: Add Your Review Checklist

- **Goal:** Create a review habit before client use.
- **Task:** Check the output for accuracy, privacy, tone, and compliance issues.
- **Tool/prompt to use:** Compliance review checklist prompt.
- **Expected outcome:** A safer, reviewed version.
- **10-minute action step:** Ask AI to review its draft for risk, then make your own final judgment.

### Day 5: Apply It To A Real Business Task

- **Goal:** Use AI on a real task with appropriate safeguards.
- **Task:** Draft a lead follow-up, seller update, buyer follow-up, listing asset, or appointment prep outline.
- **Tool/prompt to use:** The prompt that matches your selected workflow.
- **Expected outcome:** One reviewed business asset.
- **10-minute action step:** Use verified facts and remove confidential details before prompting.

### Day 6: Save What Worked

- **Goal:** Build a small personal prompt library.
- **Task:** Save the original prompt, improved prompt, final output, and notes.
- **Tool/prompt to use:** Repeatable prompt workflow.
- **Expected outcome:** One reusable workflow template.
- **10-minute action step:** Create a folder or note named "AI prompts that worked" and save the prompt.

### Day 7: Measure And Improve

- **Goal:** Decide whether the workflow helped.
- **Task:** Review time saved, quality, and follow-up results.
- **Tool/prompt to use:** Daily task planning or lead follow-up measurement prompt.
- **Expected outcome:** One improvement for next week.
- **10-minute action step:** Answer: Did this help me draft faster, communicate more clearly, or follow up more consistently?

Week-one success standard:
You do not need a complete AI system by the end of the week. You need one repeatable workflow that saves time, improves clarity, and still keeps you responsible for the final result.

## E. AI Workflow Checklist

Use this checklist to decide where AI fits into your daily business.

### Lead Response

- [ ] I can identify the lead source before drafting a response.
- [ ] I include relevant context such as inquiry type, property, area, timeline, or prior activity.
- [ ] I ask for one clear next step instead of sending a generic message.
- [ ] I review tone, accuracy, opt-out needs, and brokerage policy before sending.

### Follow-Up

- [ ] I use AI to draft follow-up that gives the person a reason to respond.
- [ ] I avoid repetitive "just checking in" messages.
- [ ] I create text, email, voicemail, or call-opener versions when useful.
- [ ] I track whether follow-up improves response rate, contact rate, and appointment rate.

### Client Communication

- [ ] I use AI to turn rough notes into clear buyer or seller communication.
- [ ] I adjust tone for the situation: warm, concise, direct, reassuring, educational, or professional.
- [ ] I review messages for accuracy, clarity, and professional judgment.
- [ ] I keep confidential information out of public tools unless authorized and safeguarded.

### Listing Prep

- [ ] I start with a verified property fact block.
- [ ] I instruct AI not to invent missing details.
- [ ] I review copy for unsupported claims, protected-class language, and advertising issues.
- [ ] I keep MLS and brokerage rules in mind before publishing.

### Buyer Prep

- [ ] I use AI to create buyer consultation agendas.
- [ ] I create discovery questions based on the buyer's goals and timeline.
- [ ] I practice objections, such as waiting for rates to drop, without making predictions.
- [ ] I prepare follow-up messages after consultations or showings.

### Seller Prep

- [ ] I use AI to organize listing appointment notes.
- [ ] I create questions about seller motivation, timing, improvements, and pricing expectations.
- [ ] I practice price conversations using verified comparable sales and market context.
- [ ] I avoid legal, financial, or predictive overclaims.

### Marketing

- [ ] I repurpose one verified fact block into listing copy, social captions, open house copy, and video talking points.
- [ ] I avoid claims that cannot be verified.
- [ ] I review all public copy for Fair Housing and advertising risk.
- [ ] I keep my personal voice in the final version.

### Operations

- [ ] I use AI to summarize calls and organize next steps.
- [ ] I use AI for daily planning and task prioritization.
- [ ] I save reusable prompts that work.
- [ ] I keep separate contexts or chats when different clients, properties, or workflows should not be mixed.

### CRM Notes

- [ ] I summarize notes into client goal, timeline, context, last interaction, and next action.
- [ ] I avoid pasting sensitive client information into public tools without safeguards.
- [ ] I use AI to draft next-best-message options.
- [ ] I make the final communication decision myself.

### Review Before Sending

- [ ] Is it true?
- [ ] Is it allowed under brokerage, MLS, advertising, and platform rules?
- [ ] Is it fair and free of protected-class or steering language?
- [ ] Does it sound like me?
- [ ] Did I verify property facts, market data, pricing, availability, and claims?
- [ ] Did I protect confidential information?

## F. Responsible AI Checklist

Intro copy:
AI can help draft, organize, summarize, and prepare. It does not replace professional judgment.

### Before You Prompt

- [ ] Remove private client names, contact information, financial details, access details, negotiation strategy, and confidential transaction context unless you have authorization and proper safeguards.
- [ ] Check brokerage policy for AI tool use.
- [ ] Use verified property, MLS, and market information when facts matter.
- [ ] Keep separate chats or contexts when mixing information could create confusion or privacy risk.

### Before You Use AI Output

- [ ] Review the content yourself before sending, publishing, posting, or adding to a CRM workflow.
- [ ] Verify property facts, price, availability, MLS data, market stats, school references, neighborhood claims, dates, and legal or financial statements.
- [ ] Remove anything AI invented or stated too confidently.
- [ ] Confirm the message fits your voice, local practice, and client situation.

### Fair Housing And Advertising Review

- [ ] Avoid protected-class language.
- [ ] Avoid discriminatory targeting.
- [ ] Avoid steering language.
- [ ] Avoid unsupported neighborhood, safety, school, or demographic claims.
- [ ] Review marketing copy for advertising rules, brokerage requirements, MLS rules, and platform requirements.

### Consumer Communication Review

- [ ] Follow consumer communication rules and opt-out requirements.
- [ ] Do not use AI to auto-send communication unless the tool, permissions, workflow, and brokerage policy clearly allow it.
- [ ] Keep records as required by brokerage policy or applicable rules.
- [ ] Make sure the message clearly represents you and your brokerage where required.

### Professional Judgment

- [ ] Treat AI as a drafting and preparation assistant.
- [ ] Do not rely on AI for legal, financial, tax, appraisal, lending, inspection, insurance, or regulatory advice.
- [ ] When a question requires professional advice outside your role, refer the client to the appropriate professional.
- [ ] If the output feels too certain, too broad, too salesy, or too clever, slow down and revise.

Quick review lens:

1. Is it true?
2. Is it allowed?
3. Is it fair?
4. Is it yours?
