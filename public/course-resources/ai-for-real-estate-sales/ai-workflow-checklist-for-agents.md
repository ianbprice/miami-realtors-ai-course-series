# AI Workflow Checklist for Real Estate Agents

Course: AI for Real Estate Sales: From Confusion to Daily Use

Use this checklist to decide where AI fits into your daily business.

## Lead Response

- [ ] I can identify the lead source before drafting a response.
- [ ] I include relevant context such as inquiry type, property, area, timeline, or prior activity.
- [ ] I ask for one clear next step instead of sending a generic message.
- [ ] I review tone, accuracy, opt-out needs, and brokerage policy before sending.

## Follow-Up

- [ ] I use AI to draft follow-up that gives the person a reason to respond.
- [ ] I avoid repetitive "just checking in" messages.
- [ ] I create text, email, voicemail, or call-opener versions when useful.
- [ ] I track whether follow-up improves response rate, contact rate, and appointment rate.

## Client Communication

- [ ] I use AI to turn rough notes into clear buyer or seller communication.
- [ ] I adjust tone for the situation: warm, concise, direct, reassuring, educational, or professional.
- [ ] I review messages for accuracy, clarity, and professional judgment.
- [ ] I keep confidential information out of public tools unless authorized and safeguarded.

## Listing Prep

- [ ] I start with a verified property fact block.
- [ ] I instruct AI not to invent missing details.
- [ ] I review copy for unsupported claims, protected-class language, and advertising issues.
- [ ] I keep MLS and brokerage rules in mind before publishing.

## Buyer Prep

- [ ] I use AI to create buyer consultation agendas.
- [ ] I create discovery questions based on the buyer's goals and timeline.
- [ ] I practice objections, such as waiting for rates to drop, without making predictions.
- [ ] I prepare follow-up messages after consultations or showings.

## Seller Prep

- [ ] I use AI to organize listing appointment notes.
- [ ] I create questions about seller motivation, timing, improvements, and pricing expectations.
- [ ] I practice price conversations using verified comparable sales and market context.
- [ ] I avoid legal, financial, or predictive overclaims.

## Marketing

- [ ] I repurpose one verified fact block into listing copy, social captions, open house copy, and video talking points.
- [ ] I avoid claims that cannot be verified.
- [ ] I review all public copy for Fair Housing and advertising risk.
- [ ] I keep my personal voice in the final version.

## Operations

- [ ] I use AI to summarize calls and organize next steps.
- [ ] I use AI for daily planning and task prioritization.
- [ ] I save reusable prompts that work.
- [ ] I keep separate contexts or chats when different clients, properties, or workflows should not be mixed.

## CRM Notes

- [ ] I summarize notes into client goal, timeline, context, last interaction, and next action.
- [ ] I avoid pasting sensitive client information into public tools without safeguards.
- [ ] I use AI to draft next-best-message options.
- [ ] I make the final communication decision myself.

## Review Before Sending

- [ ] Is it true?
- [ ] Is it allowed under brokerage, MLS, advertising, and platform rules?
- [ ] Is it fair and free of protected-class or steering language?
- [ ] Does it sound like me?
- [ ] Did I verify property facts, market data, pricing, availability, and claims?
- [ ] Did I protect confidential information?
