# AI Lead Magnets for Realtors: Attract Better Leads and Convert More Appointments

## Course Snapshot

Length: 2 hours  
Built material: 2.5 to 3 hours, so sections can expand or compress  
Audience: Realtors who want to generate more online leads with practical local content  
Format: Instructor-led demo, prompt practice, campaign examples  
Status: Future course material  

## Course Description

This class teaches Realtors how to use AI to create useful lead magnets that attract buyers, sellers, relocation prospects, and local homeowners before they raise their hand directly. Agents will learn how to build short, local, skimmable guides; promote them through social media and simple landing pages; capture contact information; and follow up in a way that starts conversations without sounding pushy.

The focus is lead generation before lead response. Agents will leave with three practical lead magnet models, reusable prompts, follow-up scripts, and a checklist for launching their first AI-assisted local guide.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, agents should be able to:

- Explain what a real estate lead magnet is and why it works.
- Use AI to create three lead magnet types: "Five Coolest Things," "Local Guide," and "Town Questions Answered."
- Use search-listening tools and local knowledge to identify useful content topics.
- Create short, skimmable lead magnets that feel local and worth opting in for.
- Build soft calls to action that start conversations.
- Repurpose one lead magnet into social posts, short videos, emails, and nurture content.
- Apply Fair Housing, privacy, and accuracy checks before publishing.

## Core 2-Hour Agenda

### 1. The Lead Generation Reset

Time: 10 minutes

Topics:

- Why many agents use AI after the lead instead of before the lead.
- Why listing descriptions are useful only after an agent has a listing.
- How useful local content can attract prospects earlier.

Humor beat: "If your entire lead strategy is posting 'just listed' and waiting, that is less a strategy and more a candlelight vigil."

### 2. What Makes a Good Lead Magnet

Time: 15 minutes

Topics:

- Useful, specific, local, and easy to consume.
- Short beats perfect.
- Local beats generic.
- Gated value: when to ask for contact information.
- Why the goal is a conversation, not just a download.

Exercise:

- Identify weak vs strong lead magnet ideas for a Miami-area agent.

### 3. Lead Magnet Model 1: Five Coolest Things

Time: 20 minutes

Use case:

- Relocation buyers, second-home buyers, lifestyle buyers, and visitors who may become future buyers.

Topics:

- Local events.
- Lifestyle perks.
- Cultural attractions.
- Seasonal highlights.
- Why people are drawn to a place before they are ready to buy.

Exercise:

- Create a "Five Coolest Things About Living in [Town]" guide outline.

### 4. Lead Magnet Model 2: Local Guide

Time: 20 minutes

Use case:

- Local credibility, social engagement, relocation interest, and community authority.

Topics:

- Restaurants.
- Coffee shops.
- Parks and local activities.
- Practical resident tips.
- Short welcome message from the agent.
- Simple call to action.

Exercise:

- Create a one-page local guide for a neighborhood or town the agent actually knows.

### 5. Lead Magnet Model 3: Town Questions Answered

Time: 25 minutes

Use case:

- Search-driven content, social posts, blog topics, YouTube videos, and evergreen nurture.

Topics:

- Finding real questions people ask about a city, town, or neighborhood.
- Using tools such as AnswerThePublic or similar search-listening tools.
- Turning question data into a guide, carousel, video, or blog series.
- Avoiding risky questions involving demographics, protected classes, or subjective neighborhood claims.

Exercise:

- Turn 8 to 12 local questions into a short guide outline with compliant answers.

### 6. Capture, Follow-Up, and Soft CTAs

Time: 15 minutes

Topics:

- Landing pages and simple forms.
- Link-in-bio tools.
- Comment-to-DM automation tools.
- Social DM, email, and text follow-up.
- Soft CTAs that invite conversation.

Examples:

- "What caught your eye in the guide?"
- "Are you already local or just starting to explore the area?"
- "Would local advice be helpful as you compare neighborhoods?"

### 7. Repurposing One Lead Magnet Into a Content Week

Time: 10 minutes

Topics:

- Social post.
- Carousel.
- Short video.
- Email newsletter.
- Blog post.
- Follow-up campaign.

Exercise:

- Convert one guide idea into five content assets.

### 8. Compliance, Accuracy, and Wrap-Up

Time: 5 minutes

Topics:

- Fair Housing review.
- Verify facts before publishing.
- Do not imply protected-class preferences.
- Avoid invented stats, school claims, and unsupported neighborhood claims.

## Flex Content

Use these if the class moves quickly:

- Build a 30-day lead magnet content calendar.
- Create a comment-to-DM automation script.
- Create a landing page outline.
- Create a simple follow-up sequence for guide downloads.
- Compare paid promotion, organic social, and link-in-bio capture.

## Practical Takeaways

Agents leave with:

- Three lead magnet models.
- Reusable AI prompts.
- Follow-up scripts.
- Soft CTA examples.
- A launch checklist.
- Compliance guardrails.
- A plan to repurpose one guide into multiple content pieces.

## Compliance and Risk Notes

- Do not use real client data in public examples.
- Do not publish AI-generated local facts without review.
- Avoid claims about demographics, protected classes, safety, crime, religion, family status, disability, or neighborhood desirability tied to protected traits.
- Treat tool pricing, platform features, and ad rules as subject to change.
