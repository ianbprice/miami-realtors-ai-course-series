# Advanced AI Prompting for Realtors

## Course Snapshot

Length: 2 hours  
Built material: 2.5 to 3 hours, so sections can expand or compress  
Audience: Realtors who already understand basic AI use and want better, repeatable results  
Format: Hands-on prompt workshop  
Status: Future course after the first three are approved  

## Course Description

This hands-on class helps Realtors move beyond basic prompts and get stronger, more specific, more professional AI outputs. Agents will learn a repeatable prompt formula, build reusable prompt templates, improve client communication, create marketing content, prepare for appointments, analyze lead notes, and refine AI outputs through follow-up prompting.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, agents should be able to:

- Use a real estate prompt formula with role, task, context, audience, tone, constraints, format, and goal.
- Create reusable prompts for common real estate tasks.
- Improve client communication, marketing, and lead follow-up.
- Use AI to role-play buyer and seller conversations.
- Analyze lead notes and identify next steps.
- Build a personal prompt library.
- Refine AI output through follow-up prompting.

## Board-Friendly Positioning

This course works best after agents have seen where AI fits into sales, lead conversion, and nurture. It is the skill-building workshop that makes the earlier courses more powerful.

## Core 2-Hour Agenda

### 1. Why Basic Prompts Produce Basic Results

Time: 15 minutes

Topics:

- Why "write me a post" produces generic content.
- Why context matters.
- Why real estate prompts need market, client, property, tone, and objective.

Exercise:

- Compare "write a listing description" with a detailed real estate prompt.

### 2. The Realtor Prompt Formula

Time: 25 minutes

Formula:

```text
You are helping me as a Realtor. I need to create [task] for [audience].
Here is the context: [details].
The tone should be [tone].
Avoid [constraints].
Format the result as [format].
The goal is [goal].
```

Topics:

- Role.
- Task.
- Context.
- Audience.
- Tone.
- Constraints.
- Format.
- Goal.

Humor beat: "The goal is not to sound like AI. The goal is to sound like you on a good coffee day."

### 3. Prompting for Client Communication

Time: 20 minutes

Exercises:

- Buyer follow-up text.
- Seller update email.
- Price reduction explanation.
- Response to a buyer who wants to wait.

### 4. Prompting for Lead Conversion

Time: 20 minutes

Topics:

- Lead qualification prompts.
- Follow-up sequence prompts.
- Lead reactivation prompts.
- Appointment-setting prompts.
- Turning CRM notes into next-step recommendations.

### 5. Prompting for Marketing and Content

Time: 20 minutes

Topics:

- Listing descriptions.
- Social captions.
- Email newsletters.
- Video scripts.
- Neighborhood guides.
- Lead magnets.
- Open house promotions.

Exercise:

- Create a listing campaign from property bullet points.

### 6. Prompting for Appointment Prep and Role-Play

Time: 20 minutes

Topics:

- Listing appointments.
- Buyer consultations.
- Objection handling.
- Discovery questions.
- Appointment agendas.
- Client education materials.

Exercise:

- Role-play a seller who wants to overprice and create a response plan.

### 7. Refining AI Output

Time: 15 minutes

Topics:

- Ask for shorter.
- Ask for warmer.
- Ask for more direct.
- Ask for less salesy.
- Ask for three versions.
- Ask for objections.
- Ask AI to critique its own answer.

Exercise:

- Take one email and refine it five ways.

## Compliance and Risk Notes

- Do not enter sensitive client details into public AI tools.
- Verify facts and claims.
- Avoid Fair Housing risk.
- Avoid invented property details.
- Avoid legal, tax, lending, and investment advice unless reviewed by the appropriate professional.
