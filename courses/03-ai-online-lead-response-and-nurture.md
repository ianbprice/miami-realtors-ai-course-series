# AI for Online Lead Response and Nurture Campaigns

## Course Snapshot

Length: 2 hours  
Built material: 2.5 to 3 hours, so sections can expand or compress  
Audience: Realtors who want a better follow-up system after an online lead enters their database  
Format: Instructor-led demo, campaign-building exercises, templates  

## Course Description

This class teaches Realtors how to use AI to improve what happens after an online lead comes in. Agents will learn how to create better first responses, segment leads, build nurture campaigns, re-engage old leads, and personalize follow-up based on buyer or seller behavior.

This course is different from the lead conversion class. Lead conversion focuses on turning interest into appointments. This class focuses on campaign structure, cadence, personalization, and long-term lead management.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, agents should be able to:

- Build AI-assisted first-response scripts for different lead types.
- Segment leads before placing them into nurture campaigns.
- Create 7-day, 30-day, and long-term follow-up sequences.
- Re-engage old leads without sounding awkward.
- Personalize messaging based on lead behavior and context.
- Combine text, email, phone, and video follow-up.
- Measure whether a nurture campaign is working.

## Board-Friendly Positioning

This class gives agents a practical campaign system. It should follow the lead conversion course because it goes deeper into the long-term follow-up agents need after the first response.

## GoHouse.ai Tie-In

Use GoHouse.ai as a brief example of how website and CRM systems can support behavior-based lead nurture. Keep it practical and light.

Good demo angle: show a synthetic lead who views multiple homes, returns to the website, and asks about payment. Then build a behavior-based follow-up campaign.

## Core 2-Hour Agenda

### 1. What Happens After the Lead Comes In

Time: 15 minutes

Topics:

- The gap between lead generation and conversion.
- Why agents lose leads after first response.
- Why "just checking in" fails.
- What a structured nurture plan should do.

Humor beat: "No lead has ever replied, 'Thank goodness, someone finally checked in.' Give them a reason to respond."

Exercise:

- Rewrite a weak "just checking in" message into a useful message with context.

### 2. First Response Framework

Time: 20 minutes

Framework:

- Acknowledge the inquiry.
- Provide something useful.
- Ask one simple next-step question.
- Create a path to a call, showing, or consultation.

Examples:

- Property inquiry.
- Website registration.
- Home valuation lead.
- Relocation lead.
- Lead with no details.

### 3. Segmenting Leads Before Nurture

Time: 20 minutes

Topics:

- Lead source.
- Buyer versus seller.
- Timeline.
- Price range.
- Location.
- Property type.
- Engagement level.
- Urgency.
- Past conversation history.

Exercise:

- Segment a sample lead list into active, warm, long-term, cold, and reactivation groups.

### 4. Build a 7-Day New Lead Campaign

Time: 20 minutes

Topics:

- Day 0 first response.
- Day 1 value follow-up.
- Day 2 question-based follow-up.
- Day 4 property or resource follow-up.
- Day 7 appointment-oriented follow-up.
- Mixing text, email, and calls.

Exercise:

- Create a 7-day campaign for a buyer who asked about one property.

### 5. Build a 30-Day Buyer Nurture Campaign

Time: 20 minutes

Topics:

- Weekly value messages.
- Market updates.
- New listing alerts.
- Buyer education.
- Financing reminders.
- Neighborhood content.
- Soft appointment asks.

Exercise:

- Create a 30-day campaign for a buyer researching homes in South Florida but not ready to book a showing.

### 6. Build a Seller Lead Nurture Campaign

Time: 20 minutes

Topics:

- Home valuation leads.
- Seller education.
- Market updates.
- Comparable sales.
- Pre-listing preparation.
- Timing conversations.
- Pricing confidence.

Exercise:

- Create a 30-day campaign for a homeowner who requested a valuation but is not ready to list.

### 7. Re-Engaging Old Leads

Time: 15 minutes

Topics:

- Why old leads still have value.
- How to avoid awkward reactivation messages.
- Fresh reasons to reconnect.
- Seasonal hooks.
- Market update hooks.
- Property-specific hooks.

Examples:

- 6-month-old buyer lead.
- Old seller valuation lead.
- Lead who stopped responding after a showing.

### 8. Behavior-Based Follow-Up and Campaign Review

Time: 10 minutes

Topics:

- Viewed multiple homes.
- Returned to website.
- Saved a property.
- Asked about schools, payment, timing, or price reductions.
- Reply rates, appointment rates, and campaign fatigue.

## Flex Content

Use these if the class moves quickly:

- Build a 90-day long-term nurture plan.
- Build a nurture sequence for someone who downloaded a local guide or lead magnet.
- Create video follow-up scripts.
- Create a reactivation campaign for a stale database.
- Rewrite robotic AI emails into a natural agent voice.
- Build a nurture campaign checklist.

## Practical Takeaways

Agents leave with:

- First-response framework.
- 7-day new lead campaign.
- 30-day buyer nurture campaign.
- 30-day seller nurture campaign.
- Old lead reactivation templates.
- Behavior-based follow-up examples.
- Campaign scorecard.

## Compliance and Risk Notes

- Respect unsubscribe and opt-out rules.
- Do not over-automate sensitive conversations.
- Verify facts before sending market, tax, HOA, financing, or property details.
- Avoid Fair Housing risk in neighborhood and buyer preference language.
