# Miami Realtors AI Course Series

# AI for Real Estate Sales: From Confusion to Daily Use

# Course Syllabus

## Course Snapshot

Length: 2 hours  
Built material: 2.5 to 3 hours, so sections can expand or compress  
Audience: Realtors who are curious about AI but not yet using it consistently  
Format: Instructor-led presentation, practical examples, group discussion, and optional hands-on prompt exercises  

Related course topics have been organized into five instructional sections to support a cohesive two-hour classroom experience.

## Course Description

This practical class helps Realtors understand how AI can fit into their daily business without needing to become technical experts. Agents will learn how to use AI for client communication, lead follow-up, listing preparation, buyer and seller conversations, market explanations, content creation, appointment preparation, and daily productivity.

The focus is practical use, professional judgment, and repeatable workflows. Agents leave with examples, reusable prompts, a simple daily AI routine, and clear guardrails for responsible use.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, agents should be able to:

- Explain what AI can and cannot do in a real estate sales environment.
- Use AI to draft emails, text messages, listing copy, client updates, and simple marketing content.
- Personalize buyer and seller communication with better context, tone, and next-step clarity.
- Prepare for appointments, objections, follow-up conversations, and client education moments.
- Turn lead notes, property facts, and client context into reviewed, agent-approved communication.
- Build a simple daily AI workflow for productivity and consistency.
- Identify basic risks involving accuracy, privacy, Fair Housing, MLS rules, advertising requirements, brokerage policy, consumer communication rules, and overreliance.

## Board-Friendly Positioning

This is the broadest and most approachable class in the series. It should be taught first because it reduces confusion and gives agents a shared foundation before moving into lead conversion, nurture strategy, and advanced prompting.

## Core 2-Hour Agenda

### 1. AI Reality Check

Time: 25 minutes

Topics:

- Why AI matters now for real estate sales, client communication, and follow-up consistency.
- Why agents do not need to be "tech people" to benefit.
- The difference between random AI use and repeatable business assistance.
- Strong AI uses: drafting, rewriting, summarizing, brainstorming, organizing, role-play, planning, and simplifying rough notes.
- AI limitations: it does not guarantee facts, replace legal judgment, replace local expertise, interpret brokerage policy, or make final professional decisions.
- Practical tool-check habits before using AI with client-facing work.
- A review lens for AI output: Is it true? Is it allowed? Is it fair? Is it yours?

Activities and examples:

- Discussion: where 20 saved minutes would matter most in an agent's week.
- Risk review exercise using a sample AI-generated real estate statement.
- Before-and-after prompt example comparing a vague prompt with a safer, more specific real estate prompt.

### 2. Client Communication

Time: 25 minutes

Topics:

- Using AI for buyer emails, seller updates, showing follow-up, listing appointment follow-up, and short text messages.
- The real estate prompt formula: role, task, context, audience, tone, constraints, format, and goal.
- Tone control for warm, concise, direct, educational, luxury, casual, or reassuring messages.
- Turning rough notes into clearer client communication while preserving agent review.
- Separate-chat or separate-context guidance when client, property, or workflow context should not be mixed.
- Reusable prompts for client messages, seller updates, text/email/voicemail versions, tone variations, and compliance review.

Live examples:

- Buyer follow-up message that provides value without making financial promises.
- Seller update after a quiet week of showings.
- Rewrite a weak "just checking in" message into a more useful follow-up with one clear question.

### 3. Lead Follow-Up

Time: 20 minutes

Topics:

- Why most follow-up fails: generic wording, slow response, lack of context, and stopping too soon.
- How AI can help with speed, personalization, consistency, and next-step clarity.
- Lead-source context, including portal leads, IDX registrations, open house leads, valuation leads, old database contacts, referrals, social media leads, sign calls, buyer leads, seller leads, and relocation leads.
- CRM templates versus AI-assisted drafts: static saved wording compared with context-aware drafts reviewed by the agent.
- Turning lead notes into next-best-message options.
- Agent review before sending, including tone, accuracy, opt-outs, and final communication decisions.
- Simple measurement: response rate, contact rate, appointment rate, and appointments per 100 leads.

Exercise:

- Build a first-response set for a sample buyer inquiry, including text message, short email, voicemail opener, and one appointment-oriented next step.

### 4. Listings and Marketing

Time: 30 minutes

Topics:

- Using AI for listing descriptions, open house invitations, seller-facing summaries, social captions, video talking points, market update explanations, and simple content drafts.
- Starting with verified property fact blocks and instructing AI not to invent missing property details.
- Using property features, improvements, showing feedback, seller goals, timing, and factual neighborhood context as prompt inputs.
- Creating multiple marketing assets from one clean fact block.
- Reviewing marketing output for unsupported claims, protected-class language, exaggerated language, MLS issues, advertising requirements, and brokerage policy.
- Preparing for buyer consultations, listing appointments, price conversations, seller updates, and appointment follow-up.
- Creating discovery questions, appointment agendas, client education points, and follow-up messages.
- Practicing common objections, including sellers who want to overprice and buyers who want to wait for rates to drop.
- Explaining market conditions in plain language while avoiding legal, financial, or predictive overclaims.

Live examples and exercises:

- Turn verified property facts into a listing paragraph, social caption, and open house invite.
- Role-play a seller who wants to overprice compared with recent comparable sales.
- Role-play a buyer who wants to wait for rates to drop.
- Create a brief appointment prep outline with agenda, discovery questions, likely objections, and follow-up message.

### 5. Daily Workflow and Guardrails

Time: 20 minutes

Topics:

- Morning planning and call preparation.
- Midday follow-up drafting and client updates.
- Afternoon listing, content, or seller update support.
- End-of-day call summaries, CRM notes, and next steps.
- Repeatable prompt workflows: save the prompt, verified fact block, tone instruction, reviewed final version, and reason it worked.
- Starter prompt library for client communication, lead follow-up, listing support, appointment preparation, and review.
- One-week implementation plan: choose one workflow, write one reusable prompt, use it three times, save what worked, and review before using with clients.

Professional guardrails:

- Protect private client information and confidential transaction context.
- Follow brokerage policy, MLS rules, advertising requirements, consumer communication rules, opt-out requirements, and applicable platform requirements.
- Avoid discriminatory targeting or language and review for Fair Housing risk.
- Verify property facts, market stats, school references, neighborhood claims, pricing details, and availability before use.
- Use AI as drafting, organizing, and preparation support, not as final professional judgment.

## Practical Takeaways

Agents leave with:

- A clear understanding of where AI fits in real estate sales.
- A practical review lens for AI-generated output.
- Reusable prompts for client communication, lead follow-up, listing support, and appointment preparation.
- Examples for buyer messages, seller updates, listing marketing, and property inquiry response.
- A daily AI workflow tied to planning, follow-up, content/client updates, and end-of-day notes.
- A starter prompt library and one-week implementation plan.
- A safety checklist for professional use.

## Compliance and Risk Notes

- Do not paste sensitive client, prospect, transaction, financial, family, medical, access, negotiation, or confidential information into public AI tools without authorization and appropriate safeguards.
- Verify property facts, market stats, legal claims, financial claims, school references, neighborhood claims, availability, pricing details, and MLS information before using AI output.
- Avoid Fair Housing risk, discriminatory targeting, steering language, protected-class language, and unsupported neighborhood claims.
- Follow brokerage policies, MLS rules, advertising rules, consumer communication rules, opt-out requirements, and applicable platform requirements.
- Do not allow AI to auto-send client or lead communication unless the tool, workflow, permissions, and brokerage policy clearly allow it.
- Use AI as drafting, organizing, and preparation support, not as final professional judgment.
