# Miami Realtors AI Course Series

This repo is the working course packet for a practical AI education series for Miami Realtors, taught by Ian Burton Price of GoHouse.ai.

The current Board direction from Letty is to build the first three as 2-hour courses, then develop Advanced AI Prompting after those are approved.

## Recommended Sequence

| Order | Course | Core Promise | Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | AI for Real Estate Sales: From Confusion to Daily Use | Make AI approachable and useful in everyday real estate sales work. | Board-ready draft |
| 2 | AI Lead Conversion: How to Turn More Online Leads Into Appointments | Help agents turn internet lead activity into conversations and appointments. | Board-ready draft |
| 3 | AI for Online Lead Response and Nurture Campaigns | Build better first-response, nurture, and reactivation campaigns. | Board-ready draft |
| 4 | Advanced AI Prompting for Realtors | Help agents get stronger, repeatable AI outputs from better prompts. | Future draft |
| 5 | AI Lead Magnets for Realtors: Attract Better Leads and Convert More Appointments | Use AI to create useful local lead magnets and convert opt-ins into conversations. | Future draft |

## Strategy

The series should feel practical, not technical. Realtors should leave each class with scripts, workflows, prompts, and examples they can use the same day.

The GoHouse.ai tie-in should be minimal and credible:

- Mention GoHouse.ai as the instructor/company context and, when helpful, as one example of an AI-assisted lead workflow.
- Keep the class vendor-neutral so it feels educational, not like a sales pitch.
- Use synthetic examples that resemble real CRM, IDX, portal, and website lead scenarios.
- Let the usefulness of the teaching build credibility without pushing the product.

## Teaching Voice

Audience assumption: busy agents, mixed tech comfort, skeptical but curious. The tone should be clear, useful, and slightly funny.

Running humor that can work without distracting:

- "AI is not your broker, attorney, lender, or therapist. It is a very fast assistant with occasional confidence issues."
- "No lead has ever replied, 'Thank goodness, someone finally checked in.' Give them a reason to respond."
- "The goal is not to sound like AI. The goal is to sound like you on a good coffee day."

## Open Source Plan

Suggested repo structure:

- Course syllabi in `courses/`
- Final slide outlines in `slides/`
- Reusable prompts in `prompts/`
- Synthetic sample leads in `examples/`
- Handouts and checklists in `handouts/`
- Source notes and public-safe summaries in `references/`

Use only synthetic lead data. Do not publish real client names, contact info, CRM exports, MLS screenshots, or private Board materials.

Do not publish private course transcripts or source PDFs unless rights are confirmed. Public repo materials should use original writing, paraphrased notes, synthetic examples, and links to public tool documentation where useful.

Recommended licensing decision:

- Course text and handouts: Creative Commons Attribution 4.0
- Code, if any: MIT License
