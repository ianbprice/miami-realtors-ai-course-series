# Lead Magnet Prompts

These prompts are designed for Realtor education. Replace bracketed text with local details and review every output before publishing.

## Safety Preamble

Use this at the beginning of any prompt that creates public-facing content:

```text
Follow Fair Housing and professional real estate advertising standards. Do not include claims or recommendations based on race, color, religion, sex, disability, familial status, national origin, or other protected classes. Avoid demographic claims, steering language, unsupported safety claims, school ranking claims, legal advice, lending advice, and invented property or market facts.
```

## Five Coolest Things Lead Magnet

```text
You are helping me as a Realtor create a short, engaging lead magnet titled "Five Coolest Things About Living in [town/city/neighborhood]."

Audience: people considering moving to the area, buying a second home, or learning more about the local lifestyle.

Include five items that make the area interesting, such as major events, lifestyle perks, cultural attractions, outdoor activities, local landmarks, or seasonal highlights.

For each item, include:
- A catchy title.
- A 1 to 2 sentence description.
- A short note about why locals or visitors enjoy it.

Make it feel local, useful, upbeat, and worth opting in for. Format it as a one-page downloadable guide.
```

## Local Guide Lead Magnet

```text
You are helping me as a Realtor create a concise one-page local guide for [town/city/neighborhood].

Audience: buyers, future residents, relocation prospects, and locals who want useful recommendations.

Include:
- A short welcome message from me as a local real estate resource.
- Three restaurants, with one line on why each is notable.
- Three coffee shops or casual meeting spots, with one line on what each is known for.
- Three local things to do, such as parks, public spaces, events, attractions, or hidden gems.
- Three practical tips for new or future residents, avoiding demographic or protected-class claims.
- A soft call to action inviting people to reach out for local real estate or home search guidance.

Keep it skimmable, friendly, and ready to place into a simple PDF.
```

## Town Questions Answered Lead Magnet

```text
I have a list of questions people ask online about [town/city/neighborhood]. Use the questions below to create a short lead magnet called "[Town] Questions Answered."

Questions:
[paste questions or search-listening data]

Instructions:
- Identify the top 8 to 12 useful real estate, relocation, lifestyle, or local information questions.
- Remove duplicates, filler, and questions that are too broad.
- Exclude questions that could create Fair Housing risk, including questions about racial makeup, demographics, religion, protected classes, or steering.
- Answer each question in 2 to 3 sentences.
- Keep the tone warm, clear, local, and professional.
- Add a soft call to action at the end for someone who wants local guidance.
```

## Lead Magnet Cover Image Prompt

```text
Create a social-media-ready cover image concept for a lead magnet about [town/city/neighborhood].

The headline should be:
"[Lead Magnet Title]"

Use a high-quality local-feeling background concept based on landmarks, lifestyle, dining, waterfront, parks, architecture, or recognizable local character. Do not list all guide questions on the image. Keep the design clean, readable, and suitable for a Facebook or Instagram post.

Describe the image concept and provide text placement, color direction, and visual style. Do not generate factual claims that need verification.
```

## Follow-Up Scripts After Download

```text
Create five short follow-up messages for someone who downloaded my guide: "[guide title]."

Channel: [Instagram DM/text/email]
Audience context: [relocation buyer/local homeowner/visitor/investor/unknown]
Goal: start a natural conversation and learn what they are interested in.

Requirements:
- Keep each message under 280 characters.
- Ask one simple question.
- Sound human and low-pressure.
- Avoid "just checking in."
- Do not ask for a phone call immediately unless it feels natural.
```

## Repurpose One Lead Magnet

```text
Turn this lead magnet idea into a one-week content plan:

Lead magnet: [title and summary]
Audience: [buyer/seller/relocation/local homeowner]
Primary market: [town/city/neighborhood]

Create:
- 3 social posts.
- 1 short video script.
- 1 email newsletter blurb.
- 1 follow-up text for people who engage.
- 1 blog or website article outline.

Keep everything local, skimmable, and compliant.
```
