# Synthetic Lead Magnet Scenarios

These examples are fictional and are safe for course demos.

## Scenario 1: Coral Gables Relocation Buyer

Lead source: Instagram comment automation  
Lead magnet: Five Coolest Things About Living in Coral Gables  
Known context: The person commented "guide" on a post and opened the download link.  
Goal: Start a conversation without pushing for a call.

First message:

```text
Glad you grabbed the Coral Gables guide. What caught your eye most: the architecture, restaurants, parks, or just the overall feel of the area?
```

Nurture idea:

- Day 1: Ask what brought Coral Gables onto their radar.
- Day 3: Send a short list of upcoming local events.
- Day 7: Offer to share a few homes that match the lifestyle they liked most.

## Scenario 2: Coconut Grove Local Guide Download

Lead source: Link-in-bio form  
Lead magnet: Local Guide to Coconut Grove  
Known context: The lead entered an email and selected "thinking about moving nearby."  
Goal: Learn whether they are local, relocating, or casually exploring.

First message:

```text
Thanks for grabbing the Coconut Grove guide. Are you already local, or are you starting to compare areas around Miami?
```

Nurture idea:

- Send a short neighborhood comparison email.
- Ask whether they care more about walkability, commute, waterfront access, or home style.
- Offer a low-pressure buyer consultation only after they engage.

## Scenario 3: Brickell Questions Answered

Lead source: Website form  
Lead magnet: Brickell Questions Answered  
Known context: The lead asked about Brickell vs Downtown Miami.  
Goal: Move from information request to useful conversation.

First message:

```text
I saw your question about Brickell vs Downtown Miami. Are you comparing them for lifestyle, commute, investment, or budget?
```

Nurture idea:

- Send a comparison-style follow-up.
- Avoid subjective claims like "best" without context.
- Offer to narrow options based on commute, building style, and monthly payment comfort.

## Scenario 4: Kendall Homeowner

Lead source: Facebook post  
Lead magnet: What Buyers Ask About Kendall Homes  
Known context: The person downloaded a seller-focused guide but has not requested a valuation.  
Goal: Open a seller conversation without assuming they are ready to list.

First message:

```text
Thanks for checking out the Kendall buyer-questions guide. Were you mostly curious about what buyers notice, or are you starting to think about timing?
```

Nurture idea:

- Send a short seller-prep checklist.
- Offer a market update if they reply.
- Do not push a listing appointment until there is a timing signal.
