## What Buyers Tend To Notice First

- Roof age, insurance history, and whether major updates are documented.
- Impact protection, windows, doors, and visible storm-readiness details.
- Kitchen, bath, flooring, lighting, and the general "move-in ready" feel.
- HOA details, fees, rules, reserves, application process, and rental restrictions where applicable.
- Outdoor space, parking, storage, pet practicality, and day-to-day convenience.
- Pricing compared with similar nearby homes, not just broad Boca headlines.

## Seller Prep Points Worth Checking Early

Before you spend money or pick a price, check these items:

- Pull permits and improvement records for major work.
- Gather HOA documents, fee details, rules, and application timelines.
- Review roof, water heater, HVAC, electrical panel, and insurance-sensitive items.
- Walk the home like a buyer: entry, light, smell, paint, floors, landscaping, and clutter.
- Compare active competition and recent sold homes inside the most relevant local pocket.
- Decide which repairs help value and which ones only delay the launch.
